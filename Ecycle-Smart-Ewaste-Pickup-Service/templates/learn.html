E-Waste Learning Center

Learn about e-waste, its environmental impact, and how proper recycling can make a difference.

What is E-Waste?

Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electronic devices that have reached the end of their useful life. These include:

Computers & Laptops

Contains circuit boards, batteries, and screens with harmful materials

Mobile Phones

Contains rare metals, batteries, and screens requiring special handling

TVs & Monitors

May contain lead, mercury, and other heavy metals in older models

Environmental Impact

Dangers of Improper Disposal

  • Toxic materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium can leak into soil and water
  • Air pollution from burning e-waste releases dioxins and other harmful chemicals
  • Workers in informal recycling sectors face severe health risks
  • Valuable resources are wasted instead of being recovered

Benefits of Proper Recycling

  • Reduces need for raw material extraction and associated environmental damage
  • Conserves valuable resources like gold, silver, copper, and rare earth metals
  • Prevents toxic substances from entering the environment
  • Reduces energy consumption compared to mining new materials

E-Waste Facts

Global Scale
  • Over 50 million tons of e-waste is generated globally each year
  • Only about 20% of global e-waste is formally recycled
  • E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream in the world
  • The value of raw materials in global e-waste exceeds $62.5 billion annually
Resource Recovery
  • 1 million cell phones can recover: 35,274 lbs of copper, 772 lbs of silver, 75 lbs of gold
  • Recycling 1 million laptops saves energy equivalent to electricity used by 3,500 homes in a year
  • For every 1 million cell phones recycled, 35,000 lbs of copper and 772 lbs of silver can be recovered

The Recycling Process

Collection

E-waste is collected from drop-off points, scheduled pickups, or collection events

Sorting

Devices are categorized by type and condition for efficient processing

Dismantling

Devices are manually or mechanically disassembled into components

Recovery

Valuable materials are extracted and processed for reuse in new products

How You Can Help

Extend Device Life
  • Maintain and repair your devices
  • Consider upgrading components instead of replacing the entire device
  • Use protective cases and screens
  • Keep software updated to maintain performance
Donate & Repurpose
  • Donate working electronics to schools or nonprofits
  • Consider selling or giving away devices that still function
  • Explore creative repurposing options for old devices
  • Participate in manufacturer take-back programs
Schedule Pickups
  • Use our e-waste pickup service
  • Ensure data is properly erased from devices
  • Group multiple items for more efficient recycling
  • Encourage friends and family to recycle responsibly
